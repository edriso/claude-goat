# Why Claude

Before we touch a single command, let's answer the honest question: why learn Claude at all, and why go deep instead of just occasionally asking it to write a function?

## Claude is a family, not one thing

"Claude" is a family of AI models made by Anthropic. You will meet them properly on the [models page](/docs/models), but the short version is that there are tiers, from fast and cheap to deeply capable, and you pick the right one for the job.

On top of the models sit real developer tools:

- **Claude Code** is an agentic coding tool that lives in your terminal and editor. It reads your codebase, edits files, runs commands, and works through multi-step tasks. This is what you will use most.
- **Agent Skills** let you teach Claude reusable know-how so it handles recurring tasks your way, automatically.
- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** connects Claude to your other tools: databases, issue trackers, design files, your own APIs.
- **The Claude API** lets you build your own apps and features powered by Claude.

## Why it matters for a web developer

Here is the difference between "I use AI sometimes" and "I work with Claude":

**Without a real workflow**, you copy code from a chat window, paste it in, fix the parts that do not fit, and lose the thread every time you switch tasks.

**With a real workflow**, Claude already knows your project conventions, edits the actual files, runs your tests to check its own work, and hands you a clean diff to review. You stay in control and move a lot faster.

## What you will be able to do

By the end of this guide you will comfortably:

- Point Claude at a codebase and have it explain, refactor, or extend it safely.
- Use planning mode so Claude solves the *right* problem before writing code.
- Automate repetitive chores with hooks and skills.
- Connect external tools so Claude can act on real data.
- Write prompts that get great results the first time, more often.

## The one idea to hold onto

AI coding tools do not replace understanding. They reward it. The developers who get the most out of Claude are the ones who know what good software looks like and can clearly describe what they want. This guide grows both your Claude skills and your engineering judgment at the same time.
