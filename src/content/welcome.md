# Welcome to Claude GOAT

This is your friendly, no-nonsense guide to getting genuinely good at working with Claude as a developer. Not the "wow, magic" phase. The "I ship better software, faster, every day" phase.

If you are new to all of this, you are in exactly the right place. Every page assumes you are smart but busy, explains the *why* before the *how*, and gives you real examples you can copy and try right now.

## What "GOAT" means here

GOAT stands for Greatest Of All Time, but here it is also a promise about how you will learn:

- **G**et hands-on fast. You will run real commands, not just read theory.
- **O**wn your workflow. You decide when Claude plans, edits, or reviews.
- **A**nchored in the official docs. When this guide and the docs disagree, trust the docs.
- **T**iny steps, big results. Small habits that compound into serious speed.

## How to use this guide

You can read it top to bottom like a course, or jump to the topic you need right now. A good first path:

1. **[Why Claude](/docs/why-claude)** so you understand the big picture.
2. **[Setup and Install](/docs/setup)** so you have the tool running.
3. **[Claude Code Overview](/docs/claude-code-overview)** to learn the daily driver.
4. Then explore **[Agent Skills](/docs/skills-intro)**, **[MCP](/docs/mcp)**, and the **[10x dev habits](/docs/prompting)**.

> New to the terminal or to AI coding tools? Do not worry. Nothing here needs you to be an expert. It only needs you to be curious.

## A quick mindset shift

The biggest jump in productivity does not come from Claude writing code for you. It comes from you learning to **direct** it well: giving clear context, choosing the right mode, verifying results, and building habits that remove friction.

Think of Claude as an extremely capable teammate who just joined your project. The better you brief it, the better it performs. This guide teaches you how to be a great teammate back.
